# Book 3, Part 15: Full Text (Chapters 115-134)

Chapter 115: The Outskirts of the Capital
o
The party takes the long way around the Worldscar, a vast chasm into the
underdark, but first they must pass through forest near Thoros.
o
Sweetie Pie puts on the ring of truth, returning his name to him temporarily.
o
They encounter a person in a black robe with a bronze mask like a peacekeeper.
They clash with the party, and their eyes begin to open as the king beneath the
mountains possesses them. The party escapes before the king can get a good look
at them.
o
The party finds the corpse of a slaver that has been attacked by ninja stars and
smoke bombs. They loot love crystals and coins from his corpse.
o
They hear commotion from Thoros, R. Mondo II is being publicly executed via
the giant bronze eye laser.
o
On the far side of the Worldscar, they run into the familiar clown from Armando’s
vision.
o
It acts erratically, like it conflicts with itself. It hands them a spell scroll for smoke
ring, Ryan’s signature spell. Bromdir ties it up, but it melts into goo, warning the
party of the castle beneath the dream.
-
Chapter 116: The Ruin-Ringed Mountain
o
The party enters the ruin ringed mountain, hoping to climb it from the inside to
avoid the laser and beholderkin that swarm the surface.
o
The party fights rust monsters, some of whom have been taken over by a sentient
fungus.
o
The ghost of a king appears and explains that the ghosts are bound to defend the
sanctuary of Algalor, unless their unfinished business is resolved, and they are
freed. That king’s unfinished business is to eradicate the myconids from his
castle’s ruin.
o
Sir Goom swears a paladin’s oath to eradicate the fungus later, in exchange for the
ghost’s help further up the mountain.
o
The party ambushes one of the fungus sovereigns, making quick work of a large
portion of the fungus.
-
Chapter 117: Where’s the Lamb Sauce?
o
The party meets the ghost of Dalton the Hungry, who’s business is to defeat the
vile goo man Cooking Pot Jack.
o
The party delves into the slime caves under the Chef’s Ruin, and fight slime. They
burst a juice pustule that fills the caves up with slime.
o
The party retreats, and is pursued by an enraged Jack, who attacks them.
o
Jack is handily defeated, despite his slime cooking techniques. Dalton’s soul is
freed, and he leaves the party an offering of stew.
o
Jhornos puts the company into the bag of holding, where there is no air, and
swims through the slime, looking for passage into the next ruin.
o
After some close calls and using Bromdir’s dust of dryness to quell the slime,
Jhornos finds the way to the Whimsical Ruin.
-
Chapter 118: The Friendly Ghost
o
In the whimsical ruin, they meet St. Broadbelly, whose business is to bring
peeking day cheer to the children of Thoros.
o
The party deems this unfeasible.
o
Bromdir gaslights Broadbelly into thinking he already ascended so he could snag
his best toy, and succeeds, regretting it immediately.
o
Broadbelly knows what all the ghost’s unfinished business is, he tells them about
the powerful armor in the molten ruins.
o
The party proceeds to the molten ruins.
-
Chapter 119: It’s Only a Drill
o
The party enters a very intact ruin of fused stone, they enter a door marked “trial
of fire.”
o
They cross a treacherous pit of lava geysers by absorbing the lava into Bromdir’s
dust of dryness.
o
They solve a maze in a furnace that heats up over time and open the vault of the
fire king. Bromdir finds a titanite upgrade shard in the maze.
o
In the vault, Jhornos fights the fire king in a sea of lava, while Bromdir and Aylan
support and fight his minions. After defeating him, his soul is freed because he
found a worthy bearer of his prized armor.
-
Chapter 120: The Nice King
o
The party encounters another robed peacekeeper, they let it open its eyes this
time.
o
The king beneath the mountains introduces himself as Chromium, god of
beholders.
o
He offers to help the party exterminate the fungus, citing his racism as proof.
o
The king is repulsed by many of the furious 5, but he leads them to the fungus
room.
o
It is a trap! The king’s minions are waiting to ambush the party. The party fights
their way through fungus soldiers to escape the spectators and gazers.
o
The party returns to their adventure, Chromium sets his minions in search of the
party.
-
Chapter 121: Lies, Tricks, and Deceit (Cont’d)
o
The party enters a place called the “crumbling ruin,” but it is actually very nice.
They are greeted by the halfling wizard Agatha Halfling, who offers to heal the
party in her Enertron.
o
Sir Goom casts Zone of Truth, Agatha refuses to speak about the Enertron while
in this spell, so the party grows suspicious.
o
Jhornos discovers that the ghost of this ruin wanted to “defeat the wizard in the
walls.”
o
Another wizard enters, Jhornos punches him, and he falls unconscious. The party
discovers these people have a jelly-like consistency; Agatha’s hat is connected by
a strand of goo to her head.
o
Bromdir lifts a carpet to look for loot, and discovers the pristine building is an
illusion cast over a crumbling ruin.
o
Bromdir sees an extra door when the illusion is lifted and opens it to see a
gyrating red ooze with hundreds of faces in it.
o
Unsettled, the party calls Greenwise to catch up and ask for assistance.
o
Greenwise updates the party on the wizard council, which now includes Jhornos’
mother, Striga Parascqual.
o
The ooze reads Jhornos’ mind while he speaks to the council.
o
Greenwise says Godrick conceded the war after being convinced by The
Alchemist that she would be the better choice to take that land, and that
Rizzmodeus plans on conceding but needs to make a deal with Jhornos who he is
contractually obligated to.
o
Striga tells Jhornos that they are dealing with an Elder Oblex, one of the most
powerful oozes and psychic monsters.
o
After the call the Oblex tries to trick Jhornos by appearing as his mother, but
Bromdir finds out the truth by poking her.
o
Bromdir and Jhornos look in the hole where the Oblex was, but it is gone. They
enter the hole with eight dwarves and the Oblex ambushes them.
o
Jhornos is possessed by the Oblex and attacks Bromdir, the dwarves attack the
Oblex, and Bromdir uses his dust of dryness to absorb a good portion of it. The
Oblex makes Jhornos put on the fool mask, nearly killing him.
o
Sweetie Pie gives Jhornos the ring of truth at the last moment, shattering the
mask.
o
A soft purple elf appears, with the ring in his hand. He takes it off, upset, and
disperses into miscellaneous gore.
o
Sweetie Pie puts the ring on one of the corpse fingers, the elf reappears. He says
he is Ilrune VII, king of Larithia.
o
He starts to melt the ring with powerful magic, but Sweetie Pie takes it from him.
The ring is now slightly warped.
o
He falls apart into gore, Frug takes it in a bag.
-
Chapter 122: Tower Scramble
o
The king of the crumbling ruin leaves the party an offering of resistance potions.
o
Bromdir finds a piece of rubble that looks like his head, naturally formed. On the
back it says “Midas” in infernal.
o
The party finds loot at the tower ruin, but also encounters more of Chromium’s
minions. They skirmish, and Chromium’s minions’ retreat.
o
The ghost of the tower ruins was cursed by the library, the party fight his angry
shadowy spirit. Peacekeeper monks attempt to meddle with the fight and kill
dwarves, but the monks are forced to retreat.
o
The party descends into the king’s ruin to explore but are ambushed by an
overwhelming number of beholderkin.
o
The dwarves rush to their aid, but before they can escape, Orin and Drod die via
spectator ray.
-
Chapter 123: The Puzzle
o
The party heads up to the Howling Ruin, the final layer of the Ruin-Ringed
Mountain.
o
The party encounters a bizarre room of shifting tiles that threaten to kill them if
assembled in a certain way.
o
Bromdir and Jhornos shut down the tiles, unsealing the gates of the ruin.
o
The party explores the haunted rooms of the howling ruin, finding no good loot.
o
The party reaches the part of the ruin where the ghosts of the kings are bound to,
Aylan casts turn undead and makes them flee.
o
The throne in this ruin belongs to Thrain Ironhand, whose unfinished business is a
genocide of elves and tritons.
-
Chapter 124: Feeling Froggy??
o
The party heads up to the temple of Algalor, but it is corrupted and decaying.
o
Algalor himself is hunched inside, blind and rotting.
o
When he hears Bromdir introduce himself, he attacks.
o
Several dwarves perish in a battle against Algalor.
o
Jhornos is swallowed and killed, but is revived by the revivify spell.
o
Jhornos kills Algalor from within his stomach, and escapes as he rapidly
decomposes.
-
Chapter 125: The Power of Racism
o
The party barricades themselves in a defunct dwarven sanctuary, and are
rediscovered by the remaining 2 ghosts, the xenophobic Thrain Ironhand, and
Brodmir from the King’s Ruin.
o
Thrain Possesses Aylan and uses him against the party.
o
Jhornos reaches out to Thrain’s consciousness, and appeals to his hatred of elves,
Bromdir slays him as he exits Aylan’s body.
o
After defeating both ghosts, the party again tries to sleep, while dealing with the
dwindling loyalty of their dwarf allies.
o
They are discovered as they rest by peacekeeper monks, which they dispatch
easily.
o
They decide the ruin is not a safe place to rest and move on.
-
Chapter 126: Cancer
o
The party finds a way up to the Iron Brain, lair of Chromium, but it requires
scaling the dangerous outside of the mountain.
o
The party finds a river that flows from a pipe out of the iron brain, the water
smells faintly like fuel.
o
Jhornos tries lighting the water on fire, it catches, spreads through the pipe, and
detonates something in the Iron Brain, causing a huge fucking explosion.
o
The party crawls through the remnants of the pipe and see spectators trying to
deal with a highly radioactive substance.
o
Bromdir runs up to it as the spectators die of radiation poisoning, in order to grab
a shiny bag. He is severely poisoned and nearly dies but gets behind cover and
drinks an elixir of health which cures him.
o
The substance the iron brain is made out of blocks radiation.
-
Chapter 127: Hunt the Hunters
o
The party enters a bizarre portion of the brain where dwarves are hunted for sport.
After proving they mean no harm, they recruit them for the dangerous mission of
taking down Chromium.
o
Bromdir investigates further, finding a statue of an eye used by Chromium for
surveillance, he uses Gouger to destroy it.
o
Bromdir
discovers
the room where dwarves are brutally processed into
peacekeeper monks.
o
Meanwhile, Jhornos is discovered by a spectator.
o
Jhornos yanks it down and attacks it with the dwarves, slaying it easily.
o
Jhornos goes to the peacekeeper room and kicks the door down onto a
peacekeeper monk while Bromdir robs them.
o
Bromdir recruits the dwarves that were being processed and Aylan heals them.
o
Bromdir finds a room where inactive peacekeepers are stored for future use.
o
Jhornos rolls a barrel of fuel into the room and explodes them.
o
The party heads upwards through a vertical shaft and hear themselves being
pursued by a monstrous Regal Aspect.
-
Chapter 128: I Am a Big Fan
o
They emerge in a huge room with a giant fan and block the shaft with the
immovable rod, the Regal Aspect attacks them with its tentacles.
o
Jhornos uses a makeshift Molotov cocktail to set the Regal Aspect on fire.
o
Chromium watches through surveillance statues and sends in minions through a
pipe.
o
Many dwarves perish.
o
Bromdir jumps onto the Regal Aspect and uses gouger to blind it.
o
Bromdir is grabbed by a tentacle and paralyzed.
o
The Regal Aspect drops Bromdir down through the shaft, and he is knocked
unconscious as he is shot out of the side of the mountain, falling to his death.
-
Chapter 129: The Final Gamble
o
The little version of Bromdir from Midas begins to glow, and transports Bromdir
to a realm out of time.
o
He is brought to a vision of his wife sick in bed, and Midas presents him with a
golden revolver and five bullets.
o
For each bullet Bromdir adds, he is given another one of his desires, the first of
which gives him wings to escape his death. He chooses 2.
o
Bromdir spins the cylinder and ends up shooting his wife, killing her.
o
Bromdir wakes up in the air with wings and the sword of his desire, he
immediately uses the sword to end his own life.
-
Chapter 130: One Man Army
o
With Bromdir likely dead, and his comrades unconscious or ill, Jhornos is
enraged.
o
Jhornos uses fuel and fire-breath to incinerate the Regal Aspect.
o
He pushes the spectators into the fan’s range with Thunder Wave, splattering them
against the ceiling.
o
One leaves for reinforcements, one keeps fighting Jhornos.
o
Jhornos stops the fan using Patricia II.
o
Jhornos goads the remaining spectator into getting physical, then defeats it.
-
Chapter 131: Battle Prep
o
Armando switches places with Rizzmodeus to allow the party to make a deal with
him.
o
Jhornos lets Rizzmodeus off the hook for his end of the Glass Citadel deal, in
exchange for Bromdir’s remains and possessions, and on the condition that
Rizzmodeus install a proper leader in his absence.
o
Evelyn, Goom, Indigo, and Aylan are all too sick or weak to continue, so they stay
back as Armando, Jhornos, and the remaining dwarves confront Chromium.
o
Notable among the dwarves was Dax, a skilled Bard of impressive bravery.
-
Chapter 132: A Dreamy Fight
o
Chromium pretends to be asleep; the party sees through his ruse.
o
The party uses their barrel of fuel to try and explode Chromium, but they are
unable to reach him in the air.
o
Chromium uses his anti-magic ray, preventing magic and his own attacks in parts
of the battlefield.
o
Armando uses the spell Fly to grant Jhornos and himself flight, attacking
Chromium head on before losing their flight to the anti-magic ray.
o
Armando hits Chromium with a powerful Fireball, wounding him greatly.
o
Chromium uses the sleep ray on himself, manifesting the world of his dreams into
reality around him. It is a nightmarish version of Thoros, featuring a black desert,
Ilrune VII’s castle, and the ominous purple sphere Nun floating huge in the sky.
o
As the party manifests allies and enemies in the dream, including a giant fusion of
all their fallen comrades. Chromium slays several dwarves, including Dax.
o
Armando gets hit with a death ray, stopping his heart.
o
Jhornos slays Chromium by throwing Patricia II at him, slicing him in half. As the
dream world fades, the party sees a vision of the castle rising from the sands, and
clowns pouring out of the shattered Orb.
o
Before he leaves the dreamworld, Jhornos manifests Bromdir and Corpium,
saying goodbye to them.
-
Chapter 133: Escaping the Iron Brain
o
Rizzmodeus grants Armando the boon of sending his ghost to Aylan, in an
attempt to wake him in time to use Revivify on Armando (1 minute).
o
Jhornos rushes Armando to Aylan, and they perform the spell successfully,
resurrecting Armando.
o
Jhornos harvests the magic goo from Chromium’s body.
o
The giant bronze eye at the top of the ruin ringed mountain falls, destroying a
large part of the iron brain.
o
As the party rushes away, they find a hidden chamber filled with black sand, with
several Reality Shards, ominous artifacts of unknown origin.
o
Frug and Hurb separate from the party, but also escape. Dirk the dwarf escapes
with the party.
-
Chapter 134: 5 Lords of Sleeping
o
Sir Goom returns to the Ruin-Ringed Mountain to complete his oath to the ghost
king.
o
Indigo departs to search for her family.
o
Aylan separates from the party as well, concerned with a personal task.
o
After the party convinces the traumatized king of Thoros that Chromium is
defeated, he bestows upon them promised land and lordships, as well as a ship to
take them north.
o
The party (and Dirk) choose to construct their keep between Half Hole and Wish
Lake. Lord Dirk is appointed by the party to oversee the construction.
o
Frug and Hurb construct their keep in the mountains near Bornir.
o
The party meets the mysterious blacksmith Gundir Battlestone at his mountain
forge. He upgrades Jhornos’ armor.
o
The party shows him their Larithian matrix, he purchases it from the party, as he
is interested in the history of that region.
o
He says that there were six recorded kings in the age of enlightenment named
Ilrune, and he and the party speculate on Ilrune VII.
o
Armando and Jhornos ask Greenwise to check on Bromdir’s family.
