# Example Input: The Crystal Key

This is an example of how to format source material for the Graphic Novel Generator.
You can use any format - this is just one approach.

---

## Characters

### Maya - The Protagonist
A young archaeologist in her late 20s with dark curly hair usually tied back.
Wears practical khaki expedition clothes and always carries her grandfather's compass.
Curious and determined, with a tendency to think out loud.

### Professor Webb - The Mentor
Elderly scholar with wild white hair and round spectacles.
Wears tweed jackets with leather elbow patches.
Brilliant but absent-minded. Maya's university advisor.

### The Guardian - The Mystery
Ancient stone golem awakened by the crystal key.
Massive humanoid form made of weathered stone blocks.
Glowing blue runes pulse across its surface.
Speaks in a deep, echoing voice.

---

## Locations

### The University Library
Victorian-era reading room with towering bookshelves.
Brass reading lamps on oak tables. Dust motes in afternoon sunlight.
Quiet, scholarly atmosphere.

### The Hidden Temple
Deep jungle ruins overgrown with vines.
Crumbling stone pillars and moss-covered steps.
Shafts of light pierce the canopy above.
Ancient and mysterious.

### The Crystal Chamber
Heart of the temple - circular room with walls of raw crystal.
Glowing formations reflect light in rainbow patterns.
Central pedestal holds the Crystal Key.
Magical and awe-inspiring.

---

## Story Outline

### Page 1: The Discovery
- Panel 1: Wide shot of university library at sunset
- Panel 2: Maya poring over ancient maps, excited expression
- Panel 3: Close-up of her finger tracing a hidden symbol
- Panel 4: Maya rushing to Professor Webb's office

### Page 2: The Revelation
- Panel 1: Professor Webb examining the map through magnifying glass
- Panel 2: His eyes go wide with recognition
- Panel 3: He pulls an old journal from a locked drawer
- Panel 4: Side by side comparison - the map matches his grandfather's sketches

DIALOGUE:
- Maya: "Professor, look at this symbol. I've never seen anything like it."
- Professor Webb: "Good heavens... I have. My grandfather spent his life searching for this place."
- Maya: "What place?"
- Professor Webb: "The Temple of the Eternal Dawn. It's real."

### Page 3: The Journey
- Splash page: Maya's silhouette against massive jungle trees
- Caption: "Three weeks later, deep in the Amazon..."

## Notes for Claude

- Art style: Adventure comic, think Indiana Jones meets Studio Ghibli
- Tone: Exciting but accessible, suitable for young adults
- Target: 12-16 pages
- Key visual moments to emphasize:
  - Maya's "eureka" moment in the library
  - First glimpse of the temple ruins
  - The Guardian awakening
  - Maya holding the glowing Crystal Key

---

This is just an example format. Claude Code can work with:
- Prose stories
- Screenplays
- D&D session transcripts
- Bullet point outlines
- Even rough notes and ideas

The key is providing enough character and location detail for consistent image generation.
